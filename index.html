From the streets of Chicago

Chief Keef

"The architec of Drill"
Unfiltered, unapologetic, and the undisputed king who shifted the culture forever. At 16, Chief Keef unleashed the raw sound of Chicago's South Side, pioneering the Drill genre that changed the cadence and aesthetic of modern hip-hop globally.


BIOGRAPHY

THE SOUTH SIDE FOUNDATION

Born Keith Cozart (1995) in Chicago's Englewood
neighborhood. At 15, while under house arrest, he
pioneered the Drill genre by uploading raw,
low-budget videos. This unfiltered documentation
of the South Side became a viral phenomenon.

THE INTERSCOPE ERUPTION

His street anthem “I Don’t Like” exploded,
catching the attention of Kanye West. The
subsequent remix instantly catapulted him to
deal with Interscope Records and dropped
the iconic debut album, Finally Rich.

GLO GANG & EVOLVING SOUND

Chief Keef later went independent, establishing
Glo Gang and relocating to Los Angeles. He
moved toward an experimental, self-produced
sound, cementing his status as an innovator.
His raw delivery and beats set the template for
global Drill and shaped modern trap music worldwide.

THE CULTURAL ARCHITECT

Chief Keef turned Glo Gang into a complete
lifestyle brand. He led the fusion of high fashion
with street aesthetics, influencing designers and
artists globally. This move consolidated his status
as a cultural figure who dictates trends.

THE INDEPENDENT TRAILBLAZER

Following his initial success, Keef prioritized
artistic independence. He focused on
self-releasing mixtapes and experimenting
with melodic sounds. This dedication to DIY
pushed the boundaries of Drill and set a
precedent for artist control.


DRILL'S GLOBAL MONUMENT

Keef's raw sound permanently altered the
hip-hop landscape. His influence is global,
inspiring subgenres like UK Drill and Brooklyn
Drill. His early tracks are foundational,
securing his legacy as the artist who globalized
the South Side sound.
