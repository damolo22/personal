From the streets of Chicago

Chief Keef

"The architec of Drill"
Unfiltered, unapologetic, and the undisputed king who shifted the culture forever. At 16, Chief Keef unleashed the raw sound of Chicago's South Side, pioneering the Drill genre that changed the cadence and aesthetic of modern hip-hop globally.


Biography

THE SOUTH SIDE FOUNDATION

Born Keith Cozart (1995) in Chicago's Englewood
neighborhood. At 15, while under house arrest, he
pioneered the Drill genre by uploading raw,
low-budget videos. This unfiltered documentation
of the South Side became a viral phenomenon.

THE INTERSCOPE ERUPTION

His street anthem “I Don’t Like” exploded,
catching the attention of Kanye West. The
subsequent remix instantly catapulted him to
deal with Interscope Records and dropped
the iconic debut album, Finally Rich..
